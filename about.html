About Me

Scientist & PhD Candidate — Fraunhofer HHI, Berlin

Professional Profile

I am a Scientist at the Fraunhofer Institute for Telecommunications, Heinrich Hertz Institute (HHI), Berlin, within the Department of Photonic Networks and Systems, where I have been working since July 2021. In parallel, I am pursuing a PhD (Dr.-Ing.) at Technische Universität Berlin (TUB) under the working title “Investigating Adaptive Baseband Techniques for Converged Optical / THz Wireless Links.”

My research focuses on high-capacity Terahertz (THz) wireless communications and their integration into next-generation 6G photonic network infrastructures. I develop advanced Digital Signal Processing (DSP) algorithms for rate-adaptive THz links and conduct experimental characterisation of photonic transmitter components — including PIN photodiode and WR3-coupled transmitter configurations — for probabilistically shaped, high-speed THz wireless transmission beyond 100 Gbit/s. I am also actively exploring neural-network-based approaches to mitigate transceiver nonlinearities in THz systems.

Alongside my research role, I have served as a lecturer for Information and Communication programmes at Berlin universities, covering topics at the intersection of communications engineering and signal processing.
